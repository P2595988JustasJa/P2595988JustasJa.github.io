INFLUENCE

"Graffiti, one of the most visible forms of crime and disorder in a community, can become a visible indicator of disobedience, social decline, and antisocial behavior among youth. As well as causing damage to the property targeted, graffiti also plays a significant role in affecting entire communities. It has been reported that communities that allow graffiti writers and street artists to decorate their shop shutters and buildings with their artwork report reduced crime rates, improved community relations, and community respect. It is possible for graffiti art to be a force for good. Having graffiti around the neighborhood creates a sense of insecurity and fear of crime. Besides being expensive, destructive, and lowering property values, this practice shows a lack of concern for the appearance of the neighborhood. In addition to telling stories, graffiti can also be used to depict moments where everything went wrong or well, to project views about people, politics, culture, art, and places in general, to express yourself anonymously, or to create another artistic expression." Art Radar Journal Since the invention of graffiti in 1970s in New York, it has spread all over the world, having an enormous cultural impact. Up untill now it has been considered as mostly a destructive tool, having a lot more negative impact instead of positive. Thankfully, both the use of graffiti and the opinion of it has improved for the better. Nowadays graffiti is finally being recognised and used as being an art medium, instead of a tool for vandalism.
